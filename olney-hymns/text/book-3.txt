<section id="hymn-1-1." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">1.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">Expostulation.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-1-1-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>No words can declare,</span>
    <span>No fancy can paint,</span>
    <span>What rage and despair,</span>
    <span>What hopeless complaint,</span>
    <span>Fill Satan's dark dwelling,</span>
    <span>The prison beneath,—</span>
    <span>What weeping and yelling</span>
    <span>And gnashing of teeth!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-1-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Yet sinners will choose</span>
    <span>This dreadful abode!</span>
    <span>Each madly pursues</span>
    <span>The dangerous road;</span>
    <span>Though God give them warning,</span>
    <span>They onward will go,</span>
    <span>They answer with scorning,</span>
    <span>And rush upon woe.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-1-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>How sad to behold</span>
    <span>The rich and the poor,</span>
    <span>The young and the old,</span>
    <span>All blindly secure!</span>
    <span>All posting to ruin,</span>
    <span>Refusing to stop.</span>
    <span>Ah! think what you're doing,</span>
    <span>While yet there is hope!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-1-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>How weak is your hand</span>
    <span>To fight with the Lord!</span>
    <span>How can you withstand</span>
    <span>The edge of his sword?</span>
    <span>What hope of escaping</span>
    <span>For those who oppose,</span>
    <span>When hell is wide gaping</span>
    <span>To swallow his foes?</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-1-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>How oft have you dared</span>
    <span>The Lord to his face!</span>
    <span>Yet still you are spared</span>
    <span>To hear of his grace.</span>
    <span>O pray for repentance</span>
    <span>And life-giving faith</span>
    <span>Before the just sentence</span>
    <span>Consign you to death.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-1-6>
 	<header>
 		<p>VI</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>It is not too late</span>
    <span>To Jesus to flee;</span>
    <span>His mercy is great,</span>
    <span>His pardon is free!</span>
    <span>His blood has such virtue</span>
    <span>For all that believe</span>
    <span>That nothing can hurt you</span>
    <span>If him you receive.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-1-2." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">2.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">Alarm.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-1-2-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Stop, poor sinner! stop and think,</span>
      <span class="i1">Before you farther go!</span>
    <span>Will you sport upon the brink</span>
      <span class="i1">Of everlasting woe?</span>
    <span>Once again I charge you, stop;</span>
    <span>For unless you warning take,</span>
    <span>Ere you are aware you drop</span>
      <span class="i1">Into the burning lake.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-2-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Say, have you an arm like God,</span>
      <span class="i1">That you his will oppose?</span>
    <span>Fear you not that iron rod</span>
      <span class="i1">With which he breaks his foes?</span>
    <span>Can you stand in that dread day</span>
    <span>When he judgment shall proclaim,</span>
    <span>And the earth shall melt away</span>
      <span class="i1">Like wax before the flame?</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-2-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Pale-faced death will quickly come</span>
      <span class="i1">To drag you to his bar;</span>
    <span>Then to hear your awful doom</span>
      <span class="i1">Will fill you with despair:</span>
    <span>All your sins will round you crowd,</span>
    <span>Sins of a blood-crimson dye;</span>
    <span>Each for vengeance crying loud,</span>
      <span class="i1">And what can you reply?</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-2-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Though your heart be made of steel,</span>
      <span class="i1">Your forehead lined with brass,</span>
    <span>God at length will make you feel</span>
      <span class="i1">He will not let you pass:</span>
    <span>Sinners then in vain will call,</span>
    <span>(Though they now despise his grace)</span>
    <span>Rocks and mountains, on us fall,</span>
      <span class="i1">And hide us from his face!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-2-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>But as yet there is a hope</span>
      <span class="i1">You may his mercy know;</span>
    <span>Though his arm is lifted up,</span>
      <span class="i1">He still forbears the blow:</span>
    <span>Twas for sinners Jesus died,</span>
    <span>Sinners he invites to come;</span>
    <span>None who come shall be denied,—</span>
      <span class="i1">He says—"There still is room."</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-1-3." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">3.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">We were once as you are.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-1-3-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Shall men pretend to pleasure</span>
      <span class="i1">Who never knew the Lord?</span>
    <span>Can all the worldling's treasure</span>
      <span class="i1">True peace of mind afford?</span>
    <span>They shall obtain this jewel</span>
      <span class="i1">In what their hearts desire</span>
    <span>When they by adding fuel</span>
      <span class="i1">Can quench the flame of fire.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-3-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Till you can bid the ocean,</span>
      <span class="i1">When furious tempests roar,</span>
    <span>Forget its wonted motion,</span>
      <span class="i1">And rage and swell no more;</span>
    <span>In vain your expectation</span>
      <span class="i1">To find content in sin;</span>
    <span>Or freedom from vexation</span>
      <span class="i1">While passions reign within.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-3-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Como, turn your thoughts to Jesus.</span>
      <span class="i1">If you would good possess;</span>
    <span>'Tis he alone that frees us</span>
      <span class="i1">From guilt and from distress:</span>
    <span>When he by faith is present</span>
      <span class="i1">The sinner's troubles cease;</span>
    <span>His ways are truly pleasant,</span>
      <span class="i1">And all his paths are peace.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-3-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Our time in sin we wasted,</span>
      <span class="i1">And fed upon the wind;</span>
    <span>Untill his love we tasted</span>
      <span class="i1">No comfort could we find:</span>
    <span>But now we stand to witness</span>
      <span class="i1">His power and grace to you;</span>
    <span>May you perceive its fitness,</span>
      <span class="i1">And call upon him too!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-3-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Our pleasure and our duty,</span>
      <span class="i1">Though opposite before,</span>
    <span>Since we have seen his beauty,</span>
      <span class="i1">Are join'd to part no more:</span>
    <span>It is our highest pleasure,</span>
      <span class="i1">No less than duty's call,</span>
    <span>To love him beyond measure,</span>
      <span class="i1">And serve him with our all.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-1-4." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">4.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">Prepare to meet God.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-1-4-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Sinner, art thou still secure?</span>
    <span>Wilt thou still refuse to pray?</span>
    <span>Can thy heart or hands endure</span>
    <span>In the Lord's avenging day?</span>
    <span>See his mighty arm is bared!</span>
    <span>Awful terrors clothe his brow!</span>
    <span>For his judgment stand prepared,</span>
    <span>Thou must either break or bow.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-4-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>At his presence nature shakes,</span>
    <span>Earth affrighted hastes to flee,</span>
    <span>Solid mountains melt like wax,</span>
    <span>What will then become of thee?</span>
    <span>Who his advent may abide?</span>
    <span>You that glory in your shame,</span>
    <span>Will you find a place to hide</span>
    <span>When the world is wrapt in flame?</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-4-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Then the rich, the great, the wise,</span>
    <span>Trembling, guilty, self-condemn'd,</span>
    <span>Must behold the wrathful eyes</span>
    <span>Of the Judge they once blasphemed.</span>
    <span>Where are now their haughty looks?</span>
    <span>Oh their horror and despair,</span>
    <span>When they see the open'd books,</span>
    <span>And their dreadful sentence hear!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-4-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Lord, prepare us by thy grace!</span>
    <span>Soon we must resign our breath,</span>
    <span>And our souls be call'd to pass</span>
    <span>Through the iron gate of death:</span>
    <span>Let us now our day improve,</span>
    <span>Listen to the gospel-voice,</span>
    <span>Seek the things that are above,</span>
    <span>Scorn the world s pretended joys.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-4-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Oh! when flesh and heart shall fail,</span>
    <span>Let thy love our spirits cheer,</span>
    <span>Strengthen d thus we shall prevail</span>
    <span>Over Satan, sin, and fear:</span>
    <span>Trusting in thy precious name,</span>
    <span>May we thus our journey end;</span>
    <span>Then our foes shall lose their aim,</span>
    <span>And the Judge will be our Friend.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-1-5." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">5.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">Invitation.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-1-5-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Sinners, hear the Saviour's call,</span>
      <span class="i1">He now is passing by;</span>
    <span>He has seen thy grievous thrall,</span>
      <span class="i1">And heard thy mournful cry.</span>
    <span>He has pardons to impart,</span>
    <span>Grace to save thee from thy fears;</span>
    <span>See the love that fills his heart,</span>
      <span class="i1">And wipe away thy tears.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-5-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Why art thou afraid to come</span>
      <span class="i1">And tell him all thy case?</span>
    <span>He will not pronounce thy doom,</span>
      <span class="i1">Nor frown thee from his face;</span>
    <span>Wilt thou fear Emmanuel?</span>
    <span>Wilt thou dread the Lamb of God,</span>
    <span>Who to save thy soul from hell,</span>
      <span class="i1">Has shed his precious blood?</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-5-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Think how on the cross he hung,</span>
      <span class="i1">Pierced with a thousand wounds!</span>
    <span>Hark from each, as with a tongue</span>
      <span class="i1">The voice of pardon sounds!</span>
    <span>See from all his bursting veins,</span>
    <span>Blood of wondrous virtue flow!</span>
    <span>Shed to wash away thy stains,</span>
      <span class="i1">And ransom thee from woe.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-5-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Though his majesty be great,</span>
      <span class="i1">His mercy is no less;</span>
    <span>Though he thy transgressions hate,</span>
      <span class="i1">He feels for thy distress:</span>
    <span>By himself the Lord has sworn,</span>
    <span>He delights not in thy death;</span>
    <span>But invites thee to return,</span>
      <span class="i1">That thou may'st live by faith.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-5-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Raise thy downcast eyes, and see</span>
      <span class="i1">What throngs his throne surround!</span>
    <span>These, though sinners once like thee,</span>
      <span class="i1">Have full salvation found:</span>
    <span>Yield not then to unbelief,</span>
    <span>While he says, "There yet is room;</span>
    <span>Though of sinners, thou art chief,</span>
      <span class="i1">Since Jesus calls thee, come.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-1-6." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">6.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">The burdened Sinner.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-1-6-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Ah! what can I do,</span>
    <span>Or where be secure?</span>
    <span>If justice pursue,</span>
    <span>What heart can endure?</span>
    <span>The heart breaks asunder,</span>
    <span>Though hard as a stone,</span>
    <span>When God speaks in thunder,</span>
    <span>And makes himself known.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-6-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>With terror I read</span>
    <span>My sins heavy score,</span>
    <span>The number exceed</span>
    <span>The sands on the shore:</span>
    <span>Guilt makes me unable</span>
    <span>To stand or to flee;</span>
    <span>So Cain murder'd Abel,</span>
    <span>And trembled like me.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-6-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Each sin, like his blood,</span>
    <span>With a terrible cry,</span>
    <span>Calls loudly on God</span>
    <span>To strike from on high:</span>
    <span>Nor can my repentance,</span>
    <span>Extorted by fear,</span>
    <span>Reverse the just sentence;</span>
    <span>'Tis just, though severe.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-6-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>The case is too plain,</span>
    <span>I had my own choice;</span>
    <span>Again, and again,</span>
    <span>I slighted his voice,—</span>
    <span>His warnings neglected,</span>
    <span>His patience abused,</span>
    <span>His gospel rejected,</span>
    <span>His mercy refused.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-6-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>And must I then go</span>
    <span>For ever to dwell</span>
    <span>In torments and woe</span>
    <span>With devils in hell?</span>
    <span>Oh where is the Saviour</span>
    <span>I scorn d in times past?</span>
    <span>His word in my favour</span>
    <span>Would save me at last.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-6-6>
 	<header>
 		<p>VI</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Lord Jesus, on thee</span>
    <span>I venture to call,</span>
    <span>Oh look upon me</span>
    <span>The vilest of all!</span>
    <span>For whom didst thou languish,</span>
    <span>And bleed on the tree?</span>
    <span>Oh pity my anguish,</span>
    <span>And say, "Twas for thee."</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-6-7>
 	<header>
 		<p>VII</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>A case such as mine</span>
    <span>Will honour thy power;</span>
    <span>All hell will repine,</span>
    <span>All heaven adore;</span>
    <span>If in condemnation</span>
    <span>Strict justice takes place,</span>
    <span>It shines in salvation</span>
    <span>More glorious through grace.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-1-7." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">7.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">Behold, I am vile.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-1-7-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
      <span class="i1">O Lord, how vile am I,</span>
      <span class="i1">Unholy and unclean!</span>
    <span>How can I dare to venture nigh</span>
      <span class="i1">With such a load of sin?</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-7-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
      <span class="i1">Is this polluted heart</span>
      <span class="i1">A dwelling fit for thee;</span>
    <span>Swarming, alas! in every part,</span>
      <span class="i1">What evils do I see!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-7-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
      <span class="i1">If I attempt to pray,</span>
      <span class="i1">And lisp thy holy name,</span>
    <span>My thoughts are hurried soon away,</span>
      <span class="i1">I know not where I am.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-7-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
      <span class="i1">If in thy word I look,</span>
      <span class="i1">Such darkness fills my mind,</span>
    <span>I only read a sealed book,</span>
      <span class="i1">But no relief can find.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-7-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
      <span class="i1">Thy gospel oft I hear,</span>
      <span class="i1">But hear it still in vain:</span>
    <span>Without desire or love or fear,</span>
      <span class="i1">I like a stone remain.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-7-6>
 	<header>
 		<p>VI</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
      <span class="i1">Myself can hardly bear</span>
      <span class="i1">This wretched heart of mine;</span>
    <span>How hateful then must it appear</span>
      <span class="i1">To those pure eyes of thine!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-7-7>
 	<header>
 		<p>VII</p>

<section id="hymn-1-33." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">33.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">The benighted Traveler.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-1-33-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Forest beasts that live by prey,</span>
    <span>Seldom show themselves by day;</span>
    <span>But when day-light is withdrawn,</span>
    <span>Then they rove and roar till dawn.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-33-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Who can tell the trav'ler's fears</span>
    <span>When their horrid yells he hears?</span>
    <span>Terror almost stops his breath,</span>
    <span>While each step he looks for death.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-33-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Thus, when Jesus is in view,</span>
    <span>Cheerful I my way pursue;</span>
    <span>Walking by my Saviour's light</span>
    <span>Nothing can my soul affright.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-33-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>But when he forbears to shine,</span>
    <span>Soon the trav'ler's case is mine;"</span>
    <span>Lost, benighted, struck with dread,</span>
    <span>What a painful path I tread!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-33-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Then my soul with terror hears,</span>
    <span>Worse than lions, wolves, or bears,</span>
    <span>Roaring loud in every part,</span>
    <span>Through the forest of my heart.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-33-6>
 	<header>
 		<p>VI</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Wrath, impatience, envy, pride,</span>
    <span>Satan and his host beside,</span>
    <span>Press around me to devour;</span>
    <span>How can I escape their power?</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-33-7>
 	<header>
 		<p>VII</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Gracious Lord, afford me light,</span>
    <span>Put these beasts of prey to flight;</span>
    <span>Let thy pow r of love be shown;</span>
    <span>Save me, for I am thine own!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-1-34." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">34.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">The Prisoner.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-1-34-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>When the poor pris'ner, through a grate,</span>
      <span class="i1">Sees others walk at large,</span>
    <span>How does he mourn his lonely state,</span>
      <span class="i1">And long for a discharge!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-34-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Thus I, confined in unbelief,</span>
      <span class="i1">My loss of freedom mourn;</span>
    <span>And spend my hours in fruitless grief</span>
      <span class="i1">Untill my Lord return.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-34-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>The beam of day, which pierces through</span>
      <span class="i1">The gloom in which I dwell,</span>
    <span>Only discloses to my view</span>
      <span class="i1">The horrors of my cell.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-34-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Ah! how my pensive spirit faints</span>
      <span class="i1">To think of former days!</span>
    <span>When I could triumph with the saints,</span>
      <span class="i1">And join their songs of praise!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-34-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>But now my joys are all cut off,</span>
      <span class="i1">In prison I am cast;</span>
    <span>And Satan, with a cruel scoff,</span>
      <span class="i1">Says, "Where's your God at last?"</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-34-6>
 	<header>
 		<p>VI</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Dear Saviour, for thy mercy s sake,</span>
      <span class="i1">My strong, my only plea,</span>
    <span>These gates and bars in pieces break,</span>
      <span class="i1">And set the pris'ner free!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-34-7>
 	<header>
 		<p>VII</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Surely my soul shall sing to thee,</span>
      <span class="i1">For liberty restored;</span>
    <span>And all thy saints admire to see</span>
      <span class="i1">The mercies of the Lord.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-1-35." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">35.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">Perplexity Relieved.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-1-35-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Uncertain how the way to find</span>
      <span class="i1">Which to salvation led,</span>
    <span>I listen'd long, with anxious mind,</span>
      <span class="i1">To hear what others said.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-35-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>When some of joys and comforts told,</span>
      <span class="i1">I fear'd that I was wrong;</span>
    <span>For I was stupid, dead, and cold—</span>
      <span class="i1">Had neither joy nor song.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-35-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>The Lord my lab ring heart relieved,</span>
      <span class="i1">And made my burden light;</span>
    <span>Then for a moment I believed,</span>
      <span class="i1">Supposing all was right.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-35-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Of fierce temptations others talk'd,</span>
      <span class="i1">Of anguish, and dismay—</span>
    <span>Through what distresses they had walk'd</span>
      <span class="i1">Before they found the way.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-35-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Ah! then I thought my hopes were vain,</span>
      <span class="i1">For I had lived at ease;</span>
    <span>I wish'd for all my fears again,</span>
      <span class="i1">To make me more like these.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-35-6>
 	<header>
 		<p>VI</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>I had my wish: the Lord disclosed</span>
      <span class="i1">The evils of my heart,</span>
    <span>And left my naked soul exposed</span>
      <span class="i1">To Satan's fiery dart.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-35-7>
 	<header>
 		<p>VII</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Alas! "I now must give it up,"</span>
      <span class="i1">I cried in deep despair;</span>
    <span>How could I dream of drawing hope</span>
      <span class="i1">From what I cannot bear?</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-35-8>
 	<header>
 		<p>VIII</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Again my Saviour brought me aid,</span>
      <span class="i1">And when he set me free,</span>
    <span>"Trust simply on my word," he said,</span>
      <span class="i1">"And leave the rest to me,"</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>

    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">36.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">Prayer answered by Crosses.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-1-36-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>I ask'd the Lord, that I might grow</span>
    <span>In faith and love and every grace,</span>
    <span>Might more of his salvation know,</span>
    <span>And seek more earnestly his face.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-36-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Twas he who taught me thus to pray,</span>
    <span>And he, I trust, has answer'd pray'r;</span>
    <span>But it has been in such a way</span>
    <span>As almost drove me to despair.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-36-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>I hoped that in some favour'd hour</span>
    <span>At once he d answer my request;</span>
    <span>And by his love's constraining power</span>
    <span>Subdue my sins and give me rest.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-36-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Instead of this, he made me feel</span>
    <span>The hidden evils of my heart;</span>
    <span>And let the angry pow'rs of hell</span>
    <span>Assault my soul in every part.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-36-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Yea more, with his own hand he seem'd</span>
    <span>Intent to aggravate my woe;</span>
    <span>Cross'd all the fair designs I schemed,</span>
    <span>Blasted my gourds, and laid me low.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-36-6>
 	<header>
 		<p>VI</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Lord, why is this, I trembling cried,</span>
    <span>Wilt them pursue thy worm to death?</span>
    <span>"'Tis in this way," the Lord replied,</span>
    <span>"I answer pray'r for grace and faith.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-36-7>
 	<header>
 		<p>VII</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>"These inward trials I employ,</span>
    <span>From self and pride to set thee free;</span>
    <span>And break thy schemes of earthly joy.</span>
    <span>That thou may st seek thy all in me."</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-1-37." epub:type="z3998:hymn">

  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">37.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">I will trust and not be afraid.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-1-37-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Begone, unbelief,</span>
    <span>My Saviour is near,</span>
    <span>And for my relief</span>
    <span>Will surely appear:</span>
    <span>By pray'r let me wrestle,</span>
    <span>And he will perform;</span>
    <span>With Christ in the vessel,</span>
    <span>I smile at the storm.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-37-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Though dark be my way,</span>
    <span>Since he is my guide,</span>
    <span>'Tis mine to obey,</span>
    <span>'Tis his to provide;</span>
    <span>Though cisterns be broken,</span>
    <span>And creatures all fail,</span>
    <span>The word he has spoken</span>
    <span>Will surely prevail.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-37-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>His love in time past</span>
    <span>Forbids me to think</span>
    <span>He'll leave me at last</span>
    <span>In trouble to sink;</span>
    <span>Each sweet Ebenezer</span>
    <span>I have in review</span>
    <span>Confirms his good pleasure</span>
    <span>To help me quite through.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-37-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Determined to save,</span>
    <span>He watch'd o'er my path,</span>
    <span>When, Satan's blind slave,</span>
    <span>I sported with death;</span>
    <span>And can he have taught me</span>
    <span>To trust in his name,</span>
    <span>And thus far have brought me,</span>
    <span>To put me to shame?</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-37-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Why should I complain</span>
    <span>Of want or distress,</span>
    <span>Temptation or pain?</span>
    <span>He told me no less:</span>
    <span>The heirs of salvation,</span>
    <span>I know from his word,</span>
    <span>Through much tribulation</span>
    <span>Must follow their Lord.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-37-6>
 	<header>
 		<p>VI</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>How bitter that cup,</span>
    <span>No heart can conceive,</span>
    <span>Which he drank quite up,</span>
    <span>That sinners might live!</span>
    <span>His way was much rougher</span>
    <span>And darker than mine;</span>
    <span>Did Jesus thus suffer,</span>
    <span>And shall I repine?</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-37-7>
 	<header>
 		<p>VII</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Since all that I meet</span>
    <span>Shall work for my good,</span>
    <span>The bitter is sweet,</span>
    <span>The med'cine is food;</span>
    <span>Though painful at present,</span>
    <span>Twill cease before long,</span>
    <span>And then, O! how pleasant</span>
    <span>The conqueror's song!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>

    <span>If he worms and sparrows feed,</span>
    <span>Clothe the grass in rich array,</span>

 	</header>

 	</header>

    <span>Through the sea a path prepared;</span>

 	</section>

 	</header>

    <span>Gideon overthrew a camp:</span>

    <span>If the end we would obtain;</span>

 	</section>

    <p epub:type="title">Not of Works.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Cowper</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-1-75-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Grace, triumphant in the throne,</span>
    <span>Scorns a rival, reigns alone;</span>
    <span>Come and bow beneath her sway,</span>
    <span>Cast your idol-works away.</span>
    <span>Works of man, when made his plea,</span>
    <span>Never shall accepted be;</span>
    <span>Fruits of pride (vain-glorious worm)</span>
    <span>Are the best he can perform.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-75-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Self, the god his soul adores,</span>
    <span>Influences all his pow'rs;</span>
    <span>Jesus is a slighted name,</span>
    <span>Self-advancement all his aim:</span>
    <span>But when God the Judge shall come,</span>
    <span>To pronounce the final doom,</span>
    <span>Then for rocks and hills to hide</span>
    <span>All his works and all his pride!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-75-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Still the boasting heart replies,</span>
    <span>What, the worthy and the wise,</span>
    <span>Friends to temperance and peace,</span>
    <span>Have not these a righteousness?</span>
    <span>Banish every vain pretence</span>
    <span>Built on human excellence;</span>
    <span>Perish every thing in man,</span>
    <span>But the grace that never can.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-1-76." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">76.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">Sin’s Deceit.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-1-76-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Sin, when view'd by Scripture light,</span>
    <span>Is a horrid, hateful sight;</span>
    <span>But when seen in Satan's glass,</span>
    <span>Then it wears a pleasing face.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-76-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>When the gospel-trumpet sounds,</span>
    <span>When I think how grace abounds,</span>
    <span>When I feel sweet peace within,</span>
    <span>Then I'd rather die than sin.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-76-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>When the cross I view by faith,</span>
    <span>Sin is madness, poison, death:</span>
    <span>Tempt me not, 'tis all in vain,</span>
    <span>Sure I ne'er can yield again.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-76-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Satan for a while debarr'd,</span>
    <span>When he finds me off my guard,</span>
    <span>Puts his glass before my eyes,</span>
    <span>Quickly other thoughts arise.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-76-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>What before excited fears,</span>
    <span>Rather pleasing now appears;</span>
    <span>If I sin, it seems so small,</span>
    <span>Or perhaps no sin at all.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-76-6>
 	<header>
 		<p>VI</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Often thus through sin's deceit,</span>
    <span>Grief and shame and loss I meet;</span>
    <span>Like a fish, my soul mistook,</span>
    <span>Saw the bait, but not the hook.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-76-7>
 	<header>
 		<p>VII</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>O my Lord, what shall I say?</span>
    <span>How can I presume to pray?</span>
    <span>Not a word have I to plead,</span>
    <span>Sins like mine are black*indeed.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-76-8>
 	<header>
 		<p>VIII</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Made by past experience wise,</span>
    <span>Let me learn thy word to prize;</span>
    <span>Taught by what I've felt before,</span>
    <span>Let me Satan's glass abhor.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-1-77." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">77.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">Are there few that shall be saved?.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-1-77-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
      <span class="i1">Destruction's dangerous road</span>
      <span class="i1">What multitudes pursue,</span>
    <span>While that which leads the soul to God</span>
      <span class="i1">Is known or sought by few.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-77-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
      <span class="i1">Believers enter in</span>
      <span class="i1">By Christ, the living gate;</span>
    <span>But they who will not leave their sin,</span>
      <span class="i1">Complain it is too strait.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-77-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
      <span class="i1">If self must be denied,</span>
      <span class="i1">And sin forsaken quite,</span>
    <span>They rather choose the way that s wide,</span>
      <span class="i1">And strive to think it right.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-77-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
      <span class="i1">Encompass'd by a throng,</span>
      <span class="i1">On numbers they depend;</span>
    <span>So many surely can't be wrong,</span>
      <span class="i1">And miss a happy end.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-77-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
      <span class="i1">But numbers are no mark</span>
      <span class="i1">That men will right be found;</span>
    <span>A few were saved in Noah s ark,</span>
      <span class="i1">For many millions drown d.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-77-6>
 	<header>
 		<p>VI</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
      <span class="i1">Obey the gospel call,</span>
      <span class="i1">And enter while you may,</span>
    <span>The flock of Christ is always small,</span>
      <span class="i1">And none are safe but they.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-77-7>
 	<header>
 		<p>VII</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
      <span class="i1">Lord, open sinners eyes</span>
      <span class="i1">Their awful state to see;</span>
    <span>And make them, ere the storm arise,</span>
      <span class="i1">To thee for safety flee.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-1-78." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">78.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">The Sluggard.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-1-78-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>The wishes that the sluggard frames,</span>
      <span class="i1">Of course must fruitless prove;</span>
    <span>With folded arms he stands and dreams,</span>
      <span class="i1">But has no heart to move.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-78-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>His field from others may be known,</span>
      <span class="i1">The fence is broken through,</span>
    <span>The ground with weeds is overgrown,</span>
      <span class="i1">And no good crop in view.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-78-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>No hardship he or toil can bear,</span>
      <span class="i1">No difficulty meet;</span>
    <span>He wastes his hours at home, for fear</span>
      <span class="i1">Of lions in the street.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-78-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>What wonder then, if sloth and sleep</span>
      <span class="i1">Distress and famine bring.</span>
    <span>Can he in harvest hope to reap,</span>
      <span class="i1">Who will not sow in spring?</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-78-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>'Tis often thus in soul concerns</span>
      <span class="i1">We gospel sluggards see;</span>
    <span>Who, if a wish would serve their turns,</span>
      <span class="i1">Might true believers be.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-78-6>
 	<header>
 		<p>VI</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>But when the preacher bids them watch,</span>
      <span class="i1">And seek and strive and pray,</span>
    <span>At every poor excuse they catch—</span>
      <span class="i1">A lion in the way.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-78-7>
 	<header>
 		<p>VII</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>To use the means of grace how loath!</span>
      <span class="i1">We call them still in vain;</span>
    <span>They yield to their beloved sloth,</span>
      <span class="i1">And fold their arms again.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-78-8>
 	<header>
 		<p>VIII</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Dear Saviour, let thy pow'r appear,</span>
      <span class="i1">The outward call to aid.</span>
    <span>These drowsy souls can only hear</span>
      <span class="i1">The voice that wakes the dead.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-1-79." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">79.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">Not in Word, but in Power.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-1-79-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>How soon the Saviour's gracious call</span>
    <span>Disarm'd the rage of bloody Saul.</span>
    <span>Jesus, the knowledge of thy name</span>
    <span>Changes the lion to a lamb.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-79-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Zaccheus, when he knew the Lord,</span>
    <span>What he had gain'd by wrong restored;</span>
    <span>And of the wealth he prized before,</span>
    <span>He gave the half to feed the poor.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-79-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>The woman who so vile had been,</span>
    <span>When brought to weep o'er pardon'd sin,</span>
    <span>Was from her evil ways estranged,</span>
    <span>And show d that grace her heart had changed.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-79-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>And can we think the power of grace</span>
    <span>Is lost by change of time and place?</span>
    <span>Then it was mighty, all allow,</span>
    <span>And is it but a notion now?</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-79-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Can they whom pride and passion sway,</span>
    <span>Who mammon and the world obey,</span>
    <span>In envy or contention live,</span>
    <span>Presume that they indeed believe?</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-79-6>
 	<header>
 		<p>VI</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>True faith unites to Christ the root,</span>
    <span>By him producing holy fruit;</span>
    <span>And they who no such fruit can show,</span>
    <span>Still on the stock of nature grow.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-1-79-7>
 	<header>
 		<p>VII</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Lord, let thy word effectual prove</span>
    <span>To work in us obedient love!</span>
    <span>And may each one who hears it, dread</span>
    <span>A name to live, and yet be dead.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
