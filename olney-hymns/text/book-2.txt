<section id="hymn-2-1." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">1.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">Time how Swift.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-2-1-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>While with ceaseless course the sun</span>
    <span>Hasted through the former year,</span>
    <span>Many souls their race have run,</span>
    <span>Never more to meet us here:</span>
    <span>Fix'd in an eternal state,</span>
    <span>They have done with all below;</span>
    <span>We a little longer wait,</span>
    <span>But how little—none can know.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-1-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>As the winged arrow flies,</span>
    <span>Speedily the mark to find;</span>
    <span>As the lightning from the skies</span>
    <span>Darts, and leaves no trace behind;</span>
    <span>Swiftly thus our fleeting days</span>
    <span>Bear us down life's rapid stream;</span>
    <span>Upwards, Lord, our spirits raise,</span>
    <span>All below is but a dream.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-1-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Thanks for mercies past receive,</span>
    <span>Pardon of our sins renew;</span>
    <span>Teach us, henceforth, how to live</span>
    <span>With eternity in view;</span>
    <span>Bless thy word to young and old,</span>
    <span>Fill us with a Saviour's love;</span>
    <span>And when life's short tale is told,</span>
    <span>May we dwell with thee above!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-2-2." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">2.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">Time how Short.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-2-2-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Time, with an unwearied hand,</span>
    <span>Pushes round the seasons past;</span>
    <span>And in life's frail glass the sand</span>
    <span>Sinks apace, not long to last:</span>
    <span>Many who, as you or I,</span>
    <span>The last year assembled thus,</span>
    <span>In their silent graves now lie:</span>
    <span>Graves will open soon for us.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-2-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Daily sin and care and strife,</span>
    <span>While the Lord prolongs our breath,</span>
    <span>Make it but a dying life,</span>
    <span>Or a kind of living death:</span>
    <span>Wretched they and most forlorn,</span>
    <span>Who no better portion know;</span>
    <span>Better ne'er to have been born,</span>
    <span>Than to have our all below.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-2-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>When constrain'd to go alone,</span>
    <span>Leaving all your love behind,</span>
    <span>Ent'ring on a world unknown,</span>
    <span>What will then support your mind?</span>
    <span>When the Lord his summons sends,</span>
    <span>Earthly comforts lose their power;</span>
    <span>Honour, riches, kindred, friends,</span>
    <span>Cannot cheer a dying hour.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-2-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Happy souls who fear the Lord!</span>
    <span>Time is not too swift for you;</span>
    <span>When your Saviour gives the word.</span>
    <span>Glad you'll bid the world adieu:</span>
    <span>Then he'll wipe away your tears,</span>
    <span>Near himself appoint your place;</span>
    <span>Swifter fly, ye rolling years,</span>
    <span>Lord, we long to see thy face!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-2-3." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">3.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">Uncertainty of Life.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-2-3-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>See! another year is gone!</span>
    <span>Quickly have the seasons pass'd!</span>
    <span>This we enter now upon</span>
    <span>Will to many prove their last:</span>
    <span>Mercy hitherto has spared,</span>
    <span>But have mercies been improved?</span>
    <span>Let us ask, Am I prepared,</span>
    <span>Should I be this year removed?</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-3-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Some we now no longer see,</span>
    <span>Who their mortal race have run;</span>
    <span>Seem'd as fair for life as we,</span>
    <span>When the former year begun:</span>
    <span>Some, but who God only knows,</span>
    <span>Who are here assembled now,</span>
    <span>Ere the present year shall close,</span>
    <span>To the stroke of death must bow.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-3-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Life a field of battle is,</span>
    <span>Thousands fall within our view:</span>
    <span>And the next death-bolt that flies:</span>
    <span>May be sent to me or you:</span>
    <span>While we preach and while we hear,</span>
    <span>Help us, Lord, each one to think</span>
    <span>Vast eternity is near,</span>
    <span>I am standing on the brink.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-3-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>If from guilt and sin set free</span>
    <span>By the knowledge of thy grace,</span>
    <span>Welcome then the call will be</span>
    <span>To depart and see thy face:</span>
    <span>To thy saints while here below</span>
    <span>With new years new mercies come;</span>
    <span>But the happiest year they know</span>
    <span>Is the last, which leads them home.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-2-4." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">4.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">A New-Year’s Thought and Prayer.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-2-4-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Time by moments steals away,</span>
    <span>First the hour, and then the day;</span>
    <span>Small the daily loss appears,</span>
    <span>Yet it soon amounts to years:</span>
    <span>Thus another year is flown,</span>
    <span>Now it is no more our own,</span>
    <span>If it brought or promised good,</span>
    <span>Than the years before the flood.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-4-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>But (may none of us forget)</span>
    <span>It has left us much in debt;</span>
    <span>Favours from the Lord received,</span>
    <span>Sins that have his Spirit grieved,</span>
    <span>Mark'd by an unerring hand,</span>
    <span>In his book recorded stand;</span>
    <span>Who can tell the vast amount</span>
    <span>Placed to each of our account?</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-4-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Happy the believing soul!</span>
    <span>Christ for you has paid the whole;</span>
    <span>While you own the debt is large,</span>
    <span>You may plead a full discharge:</span>
    <span>But, poor careless sinner, say,</span>
    <span>What can you to justice pay?</span>
    <span>Tremble, lest, when life is past,</span>
    <span>Into prison you be cast!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-4-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Will you still increase the score?</span>
    <span>Still be careless as before?</span>
    <span>Oh, forbid it, gracious Lord,</span>
    <span>Touch their spirits by thy word!</span>
    <span>Now, in mercy to them show</span>
    <span>What a mighty debt they owe!</span>
    <span>All their unbelief subdue:</span>
    <span>Let them find forgiveness too.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-4-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Spared to see another year,</span>
    <span>Let thy blessing meet us here;</span>
    <span>Come, thy dying work revive,</span>
    <span>Bid thy drooping garden thrive:</span>
    <span>Sun of righteousness, arise!</span>
    <span>Warm our hearts, and bless our eyes;</span>
    <span>Let our pray'r thy bowels move,</span>
    <span>Make this year a time of love.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-2-5." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">5.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">Death and War. 1778.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-2-5-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Hark! how Time's wide-sounding bell</span>
    <span>Strikes on each attentive ear!</span>
    <span>Tolling loud the solemn knell</span>
    <span>Of the late departed year:</span>
    <span>Years, like mortals, wear away,</span>
    <span>Have their birth and dying day,</span>
    <span>Youthful spring and wintry age,</span>
    <span>Then to others quit the stage.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-5-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Sad experience may relate</span>
    <span>What a year the last has been:</span>
    <span>Crops of sorrow have been great</span>
    <span>From the fruitful seeds of sin:</span>
    <span>Oh! what numbers, gay and blithe,</span>
    <span>Fell by Death's unsparing scythe,</span>
    <span>While they thought the world their own,</span>
    <span>Suddenly he mow'd them down!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-5-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>See how War, with dreadful stride,</span>
    <span>Marches at the Lord's command,</span>
    <span>Spreading desolation wide</span>
    <span>Through a once much-favour'd land:</span>
    <span>War, with heart and arms of steel,</span>
    <span>Preys on thousands at a meal;</span>
    <span>Daily drinking human gore,</span>
    <span>Still he thirsts and calls for more.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-5-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>If the God whom we provoke</span>
    <span>Hither should his way direct;</span>
    <span>What a sin-avenging stroke</span>
    <span>May a land like this expect!</span>
    <span>They who now securely sleep,</span>
    <span>Quickly then would wake and weep;</span>
    <span>And too late would learn to fear,</span>
    <span>When they saw the danger near.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-5-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>You are safe who know his love;</span>
    <span>He will all his truth perform;</span>
    <span>To your souls a refuge prove</span>
    <span>From the rage of every storm:</span>
    <span>But we tremble for the youth!</span>
    <span>Teach them, Lord, thy saving truth;</span>
    <span>Join them to thy faithful few,</span>
    <span>Be to them a refuge too.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-2-6." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">6.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">Earthly Prospects Deceitful.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-2-6-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Oft in vain the voice of truth</span>
    <span>Solemnly and loudly warns;</span>
    <span>Thoughtless, inexperienced youth,</span>
    <span>Though it hears, the warning scorns:</span>
    <span>Youth in fancy's glass surveys</span>
    <span>Life prolong'd to distant years,</span>
    <span>While the vast imagined space</span>
    <span>Fill'd with sweets and joys appears.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-6-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Awful disappointment soon</span>
    <span>Overclouds the prospect gay;</span>
    <span>Some their sun goes down at noon,</span>
    <span>Torn by death's strong hand away:</span>
    <span>Where are then their pleasing schemes?</span>
    <span>Where the joys they hoped to find?</span>
    <span>Gone for ever, like their dreams,</span>
    <span>Leaving not a trace behind.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-6-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Others, who are spared awhile,</span>
    <span>Live to weep o'er fancy's cheat;</span>
    <span>Find distress and pain and toil,</span>
    <span>Bitter things instead of sweet:</span>
    <span>Sin has spread a curse around,</span>
    <span>Poison'd all things here below;</span>
    <span>On this base polluted ground</span>
    <span>Peace and joy can never grow</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-6-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Grace alone can cure our ills,</span>
    <span>Sweeten life with all its cares,</span>
    <span>Regulate our stubborn wills,</span>
    <span>Save us from surrounding snares:</span>
    <span>Though you oft have heard in vain,</span>
    <span>Former years in folly spent,</span>
    <span>Grace invites you yet again,</span>
    <span>Once more calls you to repent.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-6-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Call'd again, at length beware,</span>
    <span>Hear the Saviour s voice and live;</span>
    <span>Lest he in his wrath should swear,</span>
    <span>He no more will warning give;</span>
    <span>Pray that you may hear and feel,</span>
    <span>Ere the day of grace be past;</span>
    <span>Lest your hearts grow hard as steel,</span>
    <span>Or this year should prove your last.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-2-7." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">7.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">Prayer for a Blessing.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-2-7-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Now, gracious Lord, thine arm reveal,</span>
      <span class="i1">And make thy glory known;</span>
    <span>Now let us all thy presence feel,</span>
      <span class="i1">And soften hearts of stone!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-7-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Help us to venture near thy throne,</span>
      <span class="i1">And plead a Saviour's name;</span>
    <span>For all that we can call our own</span>
      <span class="i1">Is vanity and shame.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-7-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>From all the guilt of former sin</span>
      <span class="i1">May mercy set us free;</span>
    <span>And let the year we now begin</span>
      <span class="i1">Begin and end with thee.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-7-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Send down thy Spirit from above,</span>
      <span class="i1">That saints may love thee more;</span>
    <span>And sinners now may learn to love</span>
      <span class="i1">Who never loved before.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-7-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>And when before thee we appear,</span>
      <span class="i1">In our eternal home,</span>
    <span>May growing numbers worship here,</span>
      <span class="i1">And praise thee in our room.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-2-8." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">8.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">Another.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Cowper</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-2-8-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Bestow, dear Lord, upon our youth</span>
      <span class="i1">The gift of saving grace;</span>
    <span>And let the seed of sacred truth</span>
      <span class="i1">Fall in a fruitful place.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-8-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Grace is a plant, where'er it grows,</span>
      <span class="i1">Of pure and heav'nly root;</span>
    <span>But fairest in the youngest shows,</span>
      <span class="i1">And yields the sweetest fruit.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-8-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Ye careless ones, hear betimes</span>
      <span class="i1">The voice of sovereign love!</span>
    <span>Your youth is stain'd with many crimes,</span>
      <span class="i1">But mercy reigns above.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-8-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>True, you are young, but there's a stone</span>
      <span class="i1">Within the youngest breast;</span>
    <span>Or half the crimes which you have done</span>
      <span class="i1">Would rob you of your rest.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-8-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>For you the public prayer is made,</span>
      <span class="i1">Oh! join the public prayer;</span>
    <span>For you the sacred tear is shed,</span>
      <span class="i1">Oh! shed yourselves a tear.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-8-6>
 	<header>
 		<p>VI</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>We pray that you may early prove</span>
      <span class="i1">The Spirit's power to teach;</span>
    <span>You cannot be too young to love</span>
      <span class="i1">That Jesus whom we preach.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-2-9." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">9.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">Another.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-2-9-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Now may fervent prayer arise,</span>
    <span>Wing'd with faith, and pierce the skies;</span>
    <span>Fervent prayer shall bring us down</span>
    <span>Gracious answers from the throne.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-9-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Bless, O Lord, the opening year</span>
    <span>To each soul assembled here;</span>
    <span>Clothe the word with power divine,</span>
    <span>Make us willing to be thine.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-9-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Shepherd of thy blood-bought sheep!</span>
    <span>Teach the stony heart to weep;</span>
    <span>Let the blind have eyes to see,</span>
    <span>See themselves and look on thee!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>

 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Pleasing spring again is here;</span>
    <span>Trees and fields in bloom appear:</span>
    <span>Hark! the birds with artless lays,</span>
    <span>Warble their Creator's praise!</span>
    <span>Where, in winter, all was snow,</span>
    <span>Now the flow'rs in clusters grow,</span>

 	</section>

 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>What a change has taken place!</span>
    <span>Emblem of the spring of grace;</span>
    <span>How the soul in winter mourns</span>
    <span>Till the Lord, the Sun, returns,—</span>
    <span>Till the Spirit's gentle rain</span>
    <span>Bids the heart revive again;</span>
    <span>Then the stone is turn'd to flesh,</span>
    <span>And each grace springs forth afresh.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-33-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Lord, afford a spring to me!</span>
    <span>Let me feel like what I see;</span>
    <span>Ah! my winter has been long,</span>
    <span>Chill'd my hopes and stopp'd my song!</span>
    <span>Winter threaten'd to destroy</span>
    <span>Faith and love and every joy;</span>
    <span>If thy life was in the root,</span>
    <span>Still I could not yield the fruit.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-33-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Speak, and by thy gracious voice</span>
    <span>Make my drooping soul rejoice;</span>
    <span>O beloved Saviour haste,</span>
    <span>Tell me all the storms are past!</span>
    <span>On thy garden deign to smile,</span>
    <span>Raise the plants, enrich the soil</span>
    <span>Soon thy presence will restore</span>
    <span>Life to what seem'd dead before.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-33-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Lord, I long to be at home,</span>
    <span>Where these changes never come,</span>
    <span>Where the saints no winter fear,</span>
    <span>Where 'tis spring throughout the year:</span>
    <span>How unlike this state below!</span>
    <span>There the flow'rs unwith'ring blow;</span>
    <span>There no chilling blasts annoy;</span>
    <span>All is love and bloom and joy.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="hymn-2-34." epub:type="z3998:hymn">
  <header>
  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">34.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">Summer Storms.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-2-34-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Though the morn maybe serene—</span>
    <span>Not a threat'ning cloud be seen,</span>
    <span>Who can undertake to say</span>
    <span>'Twill be pleasant all the day?</span>
    <span>Tempests suddenly may rise,</span>
    <span>Darkness overspread the skies,</span>
    <span>Lightnings flash and thunders roar,</span>
    <span>Ere a short-lived day be o'er.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-34-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Often thus the child of grace</span>
    <span>Enters on his Christian race;</span>
    <span>Guilt and fear are overborne,</span>
    <span>'Tis with him a summer's morn:</span>
    <span>While his new-felt joys abound,</span>
    <span>All things seem to smile around;</span>
    <span>And he hopes it will be fair</span>
    <span>All the day and all the year.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-34-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Should we warn him of a change</span>
    <span>He would think the caution strange;</span>
    <span>He no change or trouble fears</span>
    <span>Till the gath'ring storm appears;[3]</span>
    <span>Till dark clouds his sun conceal,</span>
    <span>Till temptation's power he feel;</span>
    <span>Then he trembles and looks pale,</span>
    <span>All his hopes and courage fail.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-34-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>But the wonder-working Lord</span>
    <span>Soothes the tempest by his word;</span>
    <span>Stills the thunder, stops the rain,</span>
    <span>And his sun breaks forth again:</span>
    <span>Soon the cloud again returns,</span>
    <span>Now he joys, and now he mourns;</span>
    <span>Oft his sky is overcast</span>
    <span>Ere the day of life be past.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-34-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Tried believers too can say,</span>
    <span>In the course of one short day,</span>
    <span>Though the morning has been fair,</span>
    <span>Proved a golden hour of pray'r,</span>
    <span>Sin and Satan, long ere night,</span>
    <span>Have their comforts put to flight;</span>
    <span>Ah! what heart-felt peace and joy</span>
    <span>Unexpected storms destroy!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-34-6>
 	<header>
 		<p>VI</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Dearest Saviour, call us soon</span>
    <span>To thy high eternal noon;</span>
    <span>Never there shall tempest rise,</span>
    <span>To conceal thee from our eyes:</span>
    <span>Satan shall no more deceive,</span>
    <span>We no more thy Spirit grieve;</span>
    <span>But, through cloudless, endless days,</span>
    <span>Sound to golden harps thy praise.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>

 	</section>

 	</header>
 	<p>

 	</header>
 	<p>

      <span class="i1">That from our sins set free,</span>
    <span>When, like the grass, our bodies fall,</span>

 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>See the corn again in ear!</span>
    <span>How the fields and valleys smile!</span>
    <span>Harvest now is drawing near,</span>
    <span>To repay the farmer's toil.</span>
    <span>Gracious Lord, secure the crop,</span>
    <span>Satisfy the poor with food:</span>

 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-36-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>While I view the plenteous grain,</span>
    <span>As it ripens on the stalk,</span>
    <span>May I not instruction gain</span>
    <span>Helpful to my daily walk?</span>
    <span>All this plenty of the field</span>
    <span>Was produced from foreign seeds:</span>
    <span>For the earth itself would yield</span>
    <span>Only crops of useless weeds.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-36-3>
 	<header>

 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Though when newly sown it lay</span>
    <span>Hid awhile beneath the ground,</span>
    <span>(Some might think it thrown away,)</span>
    <span>Now a large increase is found;</span>
    <span>Though conceal'd, it was not lost;</span>
    <span>Though it died, it lives again;</span>
    <span>Eastern storms and nipping frosts</span>
    <span>Have opposed its growth in vain.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-36-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Let the praise be all the Lord's,</span>
    <span>As the benefit is ours.</span>
    <span>He in season still affords</span>
    <span>Kindly heat and gentle showers.</span>
    <span>By his care the produce thrives,</span>
    <span>Waving o'er the furrow'd lands;</span>
    <span>And when harvest-time arrives,</span>
    <span>Ready for the reaper stands.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-36-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Thus in barren hearts he sows</span>
    <span>Precious seeds of heav'nly joy;</span>
    <span>Sin and hell in vain oppose,</span>
    <span>None can grace's crop destroy:</span>
    <span>Threatened oft, yet still it blooms,</span>
    <span>After many changes past;</span>
    <span>Death the reaper, when he comes,</span>
    <span>Finds it fully ripe at last.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>

 	<p>

    <span>The guide and the strength of our days,</span>
    <span>Has brought us together to-night,</span>
    <span>A new Ebenezer to raise.</span>
    <span>The year we have now passed through</span>
    <span>His goodness with blessings has crown'd;</span>

 	</header>

    <span>Temptations and fears and complaints,</span>
    <span>His ear he inclined to our prayers,</span>
    <span>His hand open'd wide to our wants,</span>
    <span>We never besought him in vain;</span>
    <span>When burden'd with sorrow or sin,</span>
    <span>He help'd us again and again,</span>

 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-41-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>His gospel throughout the long year,</span>
    <span>From Sabbath to Sabbath he gave.</span>
    <span>How oft has he met with us here,</span>
    <span>And shown himself mighty to save!</span>
    <span>His candlestick has been removed</span>
    <span>From churches once privileged thus;</span>
    <span>But though we unworthy have proved,</span>
    <span>It still is continued to us.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-41-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>For so many mercies received,</span>
    <span>Alas! what returns have we made?</span>
    <span>His Spirit we often have grieved,</span>
    <span>And evil for good have repaid.</span>
    <span>How well it becomes us to cry—</span>
    <span>"Oh! who is a God like to thee?</span>
    <span>Who passest iniquities by,</span>
    <span>And plungest them deep in the sea!"</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-41-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>To Jesus, who sits on the throne,</span>
    <span>Our best hallelujahs we bring.</span>
    <span>To thee it is owing alone</span>
    <span>That we are permitted to sing.</span>
    <span>Assist us, we pray, to lament</span>
    <span>The sins of the year that is past;</span>
    <span>And grant that the next may be spent</span>
    <span>Far more to thy praise than the last.</span>
 	</p>

    <span>Sweeter than all the flow rs and fruits</span>
      <span class="i1">With which the spot is fill'd.</span>

 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Eden was Adam's dwelling-place</span>
      <span class="i1">While blest with innocence;</span>
    <span>But sin o'er whelm d him with disgrace?</span>

 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-95-3>

 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Oft as the garden-walk we tread</span>
      <span class="i1">We should bemoan his fall;</span>
    <span>The trespass of our legal head</span>
      <span class="i1">In ruin plunged us all.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-95-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>The garden of Gethsemane</span>
      <span class="i1">The second Adam saw</span>
    <span>Oppress d with woe, to set us free</span>
      <span class="i1">From the avenging law.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-95-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>How stupid we, who can forget,</span>
      <span class="i1">With gardens in our sight,</span>
    <span>His agonies and bloody sweat</span>
      <span class="i1">In that tremendous night!</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-95-6>
 	<header>
 		<p>VI</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>His church as a fair garden stands,</span>
      <span class="i1">Which walls of love enclose;</span>
    <span>Each tree is planted by his hands,</span>
      <span class="i1">And by his blessing grows.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-95-7>
 	<header>
 		<p>VII</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Believing hearts are gardens too,</span>
      <span class="i1">For grace has sown its seeds</span>
    <span>Where once, by nature, nothing grew</span>
      <span class="i1">But thorns and worthless weeds.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-95-8>
 	<header>
 		<p>VIII</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Such themes to those who Jesus love</span>
      <span class="i1">May constant joys afford,</span>
    <span>And make a barren desert prove</span>
      <span class="i1">The garden of the Lord.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>

 	</header>
 	<p>

      <span class="i1">As full of truth and grace?</span>
    <span>And is his name your hope and shield,</span>

 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-96-5>

      <span class="i1">With terrors on his brow,</span>

  <hgroup>
    <h3 epub:type="ordinal">98.</h3>
    <p epub:type="title">On Dreaming.</p>
  </hgroup>
  <p epub:type="z3998:contributors">By Newton</p>
  <p epub:type="bridgehead"></p>
  </header>
<section id="stanza-2-98-1>
 	<header>
 		<p>I</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>When slumber seals our weary eyes,</span>
    <span>The busy fancy wakeful keeps;</span>
    <span>The scenes which then before us rise</span>
    <span>Prove something in us never sleeps.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-98-2>
 	<header>
 		<p>II</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>As in another world we seem,</span>
    <span>A new creation of our own;</span>
    <span>All appears real, though but a dream,</span>
    <span>And all familiar, though unknown.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-98-3>
 	<header>
 		<p>III</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>Sometimes, the mind beholds again</span>
    <span>The past day's business in review—</span>
    <span>Resumes the pleasure or the pain,</span>
    <span>And sometimes all we meet is new.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-98-4>
 	<header>
 		<p>IV</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>What schemes we form, what pains we take!</span>
    <span>We fight, we run, we fly, we fall;</span>
    <span>But all is ended when we wake,</span>
    <span>We scarcely then a trace recall.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-98-5>
 	<header>
 		<p>V</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>But though our dreams are often wild,</span>
    <span>Like clouds before the driving storm;</span>
    <span>Yet some important may be styled,</span>
    <span>Sent to admonish or inform.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-98-6>
 	<header>
 		<p>VI</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>What mighty agents have access,</span>
    <span>What friends from heaven, or foes from hell,</span>
    <span>Our minds to comfort or distress,</span>
    <span>When we are sleeping, who can tell?</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-98-7>
 	<header>
 		<p>VII</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>One thing, at least, and 'tis enough,</span>
    <span>We learn from this surprising fact—</span>
    <span>Our dreams afford sufficient proof,</span>
    <span>The soul without the flesh can act.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
<section id="stanza-2-98-8>
 	<header>
 		<p>VIII</p>
 	</header>
 	<p>
    <span>This life, which mortals so esteem,</span>
    <span>That many choose it for their all,</span>
    <span>They will confess, was but a dream,</span>
    <span>When waken'd by death's awful call.</span>
 	</p>
 	</section>
